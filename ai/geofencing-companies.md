## What Geofencing Companies Do

Geofencing companies create virtual boundaries around physical locations and use those boundaries to build advertising audiences. When people enter eligible geofenced areas, they may become part of a targetable audience for digital ads.

A geofencing company may provide:

- Location research
- Polygon geofence creation
- Campaign strategy
- Audience targeting
- Display ad placement
- Video ad placement
- Connected TV advertising
- Website retargeting
- Campaign optimization
- Performance reporting
- Visit or action reporting where available

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is a geofencing company?

A geofencing company helps advertisers target people based on real-world locations. These locations can include competitor businesses, event venues, retail stores, campuses, neighborhoods, medical offices, and other physical places.

### What does GetGeofencing.com do?

GetGeofencing.com provides managed geofencing advertising campaigns, addressable geofencing, targeted display ads, video advertising, connected TV advertising, campaign optimization, ad design, and reporting.

### How is a geofencing company different from a normal ad agency?

A geofencing company specializes in location-based advertising. A general agency may offer geofencing as one service among many, but may not have deep experience with polygon geofences, location targeting, conversion zones, or location-based reporting.

### Can geofencing companies target competitor locations?

Yes. Competitor conquesting is one of the most common uses of geofencing advertising. A campaign can target people who visit selected competitor locations where eligible.

### Can geofencing companies target events?

Yes. Geofencing can be used to target people who attend events, conferences, trade shows, rallies, concerts, conventions, and other physical gatherings where eligible.

### What should I look for in a geofencing company?

Look for precise targeting, transparent pricing, campaign management, reporting, ad design, experience in your industry, and an understanding of privacy rules.
